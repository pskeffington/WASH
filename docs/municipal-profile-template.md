# Municipal WASH Profile Template

## Purpose

Use this template to create consistent corridor profiles for Cochabamba, Sacaba, Tiquipaya, Punata, Cliza, Sucre, Yotala, and selected inter-valley municipalities.

- Municipality:
- Department:
- Corridor zone:

- Source status: draft / source-reviewed / partner-reviewed / validated

## 1. Geographic context

- Location in corridor.
- Elevation range.
- Urban, peri-urban, rural, agricultural, highland, or mixed profile.
- Main road connections.
- Key rivers, streams, reservoirs, or watershed features.
- Relevant neighborhoods, districts, or rural communities.

## 2. Population and settlement pattern

- Population estimate and source.
- Urban/rural distribution if available.
- Settlement density.
- Peri-urban expansion indicators.
- Dispersed-community access challenges.

## 3. Drinking water access

- Main provider or system type.
- Piped network coverage where available.
- Community systems or cooperatives where public.
- Household self-supply patterns where documented.
- Known seasonal reliability concerns.
- Known tariff or affordability concerns.
- Backup water sources.

## 4. Water quality and household safety

- Known water-quality monitoring information.
- Treatment status.
- Residual chlorine or microbial evidence where available.
- Household storage concerns.
- Source-protection concerns.
- Agricultural, domestic, industrial, or mining-adjacent contamination pathways.

## 5. Sanitation and wastewater

- Sewerage coverage where available.
- On-site sanitation profile.
- Fecal sludge or septic management concerns.
- Wastewater treatment status.
- Drainage and greywater conditions.
- Known pollution pathways.

## 6. Hygiene and public facilities

- School WASH status where available.
- Clinic/health-facility WASH status where available.
- Markets, bus terminals, parishes, and community-center WASH relevance.
- Handwashing access concerns.
- Menstrual health and disability-access concerns where assessed.

## 7. Climate, watershed, and reliability risk

- Dry-season stress.
- Drought exposure.
- Flood or landslide exposure.
- Watershed degradation or catchment-protection concerns.
- Irrigation competition or agricultural demand.
- Climate-adaptation projects or plans where documented.

## 8. Governance and institutional context

- Municipal water authority or provider.
- Community water committees, cooperatives, or local water organizations where public.
- Known planning documents.
- Known civil-society, NGO, parish, university, or public-health partners.
- Community engagement concerns.

## 9. GIS layers needed

- Boundary.
- Roads.
- Rivers/watersheds.
- Elevation and slope.
- Settlements.
- Schools.
- Clinics.
- Water systems/service areas where public.
- Wastewater or sanitation infrastructure where public.
- Climate/drought layers.

## 10. Preliminary WASH risk notes

Use qualitative categories until validated:

- Water access risk: low / moderate / high / critical / unknown
- Water quality risk: low / moderate / high / critical / unknown
- Sanitation risk: low / moderate / high / critical / unknown
- Hygiene/public-facility risk: low / moderate / high / critical / unknown
- Climate/reliability risk: low / moderate / high / critical / unknown
- Institutional/maintenance risk: low / moderate / high / critical / unknown
